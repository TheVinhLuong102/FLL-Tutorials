For this weekend's #hardwarejam challenge from the Facebook group (https://www.facebook.com/groups/hardw...) we had to make a track at 80bpm or slower, and play it live!

My entry is played primarily on the Digitone (I love it's synth sounds), with a beat provided by the Digitakt. I've got the MicroMonsta set up as a drone note for part of the track, sequenced by the MIDI channels on the Digitakt.

Recorded live at my kitchen counter through Behringer UMC404HD audio interface i...nto Ableton. No mixing or mastering done, so this is as I played it, fumbles and all!

Elektron Digitone
Elektron Digitakt
AudioThingies MicroMonsta
Behringer UMC404HD audio interface

SynthDad
· June 2 ·

I was playing around with the effects and delays on my Soundcraft mixing desk, so I decided to make a short video showcasing some of them. Well that short video became a 10 minute long video! I hope you find it useful...

I use an AudioThingies MicroMonsta to test out the delay and reverb effects on the Soundcraft Signature MTK 12. In this video I talk through how I've configured the channels on the Soundcraft to route audio from the MicroMonsta to the FX processor in the desk.

The Signature MTK 12 is both a mixer and an audio interface. It provides all 12 input channels over USB into your DAW, and it offers channel 13/14 as an additional stereo output which is the final mix off the desk. So you have a choice of mixing each individual instrument in your DAW, or doing a live mix on the desk itself. Nice!

SynthDad
· April 1 ·

A short afternoon jam with two Pocket Operators, Digitone and Digitakt, all slaved to a MIDI and sync clock from the Arturia Keystep.

The PO-12 and PO-16 integrate very nicely with the Elektron devices. I've got the Keystep set up as the MIDI master routing into the Digitone, and then out the MIDI thru port into the Digitakt.

The Keystep also sends out a sync click track to each PO. I'm using a nifty sync splitter I bought on Tindie (https://www.tindie.com/products/p0k3t...)... and the audio from each PO goes into the DT and DN.

I set the BPM to 100 on the Keystep and played around to chain some patterns together, overlaying stock patterns from the two POs on top. The Digitone has patterns from my previous two videos (Euclidean rhythms and Arp Driver) and the Digitakt has some random drum tracks and samples.

Let me know what you think!
